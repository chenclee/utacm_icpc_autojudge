Dealer

I have to admit, you're a pretty good programmer, but you're pretty freakin' bad at cards.

The game you and your rivals play is a simple game. Some number of cards are dealt by a robot to everyone. Everyone gets the same amount of cards. Before the robot passes out the cards, someone shuffles the cards and gives them to the robot. Then, the robot makes sure no one cheated by ensuring all cards are there. Unfortunately, you don't know how to shuffle, but everyone else does. You haven't won a single game in your life, and have you have no idea why...

There are currently K people playing the game, including you. If there are N cards, then N must be divisible by K, and everyone gets N / K cards. The cards are also labeled 1, 2, ..., N. Someone wins if the sum of their cards is greater than everyone else's hand sum.

You're sick and tired of losing. You've lost millions to these crooks. You decide to hack the robot when everyone leaves. Luckily for you, the robot stores the location of every card right before it deals. You have to figure out how to use this to your advantage...

You somehow managed to get the robot to second hand deal. What does this mean? It means that the robot can deal the second card from the top instead of the first one. However, your hardware hack is buggy, so once it second hand deals, it has to normally pass out D cards before having the option of doing it again.

You don't want to play a round that you won't win. So, you've programmed the robot to message you when you'll lose. However, you still need to figure out if you can win or lose a round given the hacks you've made. Write that program.

The robot always deals to the left first, and you will always be the first to the left of the robot. Also, the robot must deal D cards before being able to perform its first second hand deal.

Input

The first line of input will contain a single integer T, the number of test cases. The first line of each test case has three integers, N, K, and D. N is the number of cards, K is the number of people playing. D is the number of turns the robot must wait to second hand deal again. The following line has N integers, the ordering of the N cards.

Constraints

There are at most 10 test cases. Neither N, M, or D will exceed 1,000. N and M will be positive, but D may be 0.

Output

For each test case, print YES if you can win that round; otherwise print NO.

Sample Input

3
4 2 2
1 4 3 2
6 3 6
6 5 4 1 2 3
4 2 1
3 2 1 4

Sample Output

NO
NO
YES

Explanation of Sample I/O

For test case 1, the robot has to wait until 1 and 4 has been passed, since D = 2. That means that you have 1 and your only opponent has 4. No matter if you get 2 or 3 as your next card, you won't beat your opponent's two cards.

For test case 2, you can never second hand deal because D = 6, but you'll tie with everyone else this hand anyway. So, you won't win.

For test case 3, You get the 3 by default. You let your opponent have the 2. You second hand deal to get the 4. Your opponent takes the 1. You have a hand sum of 7 and they of 3, so you win.
