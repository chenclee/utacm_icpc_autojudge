Bully

School is rough as it is. It's no better when you have a bully...

Unfortunately, the Prime bully has chosen you to be his victim. Each day, before lunch, he will come up to you and take away a fraction of your money. He selects a random prime number, P, that divides the number of dollars you have, D, and then takes away D/P dollars for you.

On top of that burden, you still have to pay for lunch at a total cost of L dollars. Assuming the best case scenario, what's the maximum number of days you could pay for lunch?

Input

The first line of input will contain a single integer T, the number of test cases you have
The first and only line of each test case contains two integers: D, the amount of dollars you have initially, and L, the cost of daily lunch in dollars.

Constraints

1 ≤ T ≤ 10
1 ≤ D ≤ 1000
2 ≤ L ≤ 1000

Output

For each test case, print the maximum number of days you could pay for lunch.

Sample Input

2
10 2
5 4

Sample Output

3
1
